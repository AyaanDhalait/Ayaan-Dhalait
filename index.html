About Me

My journey into programming began when I was just 5 years old. That’s when I was first introduced to Scratch. At that age, it wasn’t about writing code—it was about understanding logic, sequencing, and creativity. Scratch helped me develop the core mindset of a programmer: breaking ideas into steps and turning imagination into something interactive.

By the time I turned 9, my interests had shifted toward real game engines and professional tools. I started working with Unity and learned C#, diving into game mechanics, physics systems, and scripting. This was my first exposure to industry-grade development, and it taught me how complex real software can be.

However, at around 9½ years old, I experienced what I now understand as burnout. Unity felt overwhelming, and I stepped back from it. I continued learning C# independently for about a month, focusing on understanding the language itself rather than building large projects. This phase taught me an important lesson about pacing, balance, and sustainable learning.

From around 10 to 11 years old, I explored Java. Java helped me strengthen my understanding of object-oriented programming, structured logic, and disciplined coding practices. This period played a key role in shaping how I design software systems today.

At 11 years old, I entered the world of the web by learning HTML. What started as simple pages quickly evolved into a deeper interest in how the internet works. Between the ages of 11 and 13, I extensively worked with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, building websites, experimenting with layouts, responsiveness, and client-side logic.

Also at 11, I discovered Python—the language that would become my strongest tool. From 11 to 13, I used Python for a wide range of applications, including scripting, automation, game development, logical problem-solving, and full projects. Python allowed me to focus on ideas rather than syntax, making it a perfect fit for rapid development and experimentation.

Between 11 and 13, I didn’t limit myself to a single stack. I continuously explored new technologies, frameworks, and tools, combining backend logic with frontend development and experimenting with different programming paradigms. This period shaped me into a versatile, full-stack-oriented developer with a strong foundation in both theory and practice.

Looking back, each phase—from Scratch to Unity, from burnout to recovery, from Java to Python and web technologies— played a critical role in shaping how I think, learn, and build today. Every step, including setbacks, contributed to my growth as a developer and problem solver.
